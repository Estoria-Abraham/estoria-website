The classic California exterior, done classically.

From a hairline crack repair to a full three-coat re-stucco, we work the trowel by hand — in three coats, with the patience that texture demands.

A traditional finish on a modern wall.

Stucco is the most honest exterior in the world. There's nowhere to hide. A bad lath job shows. A floated coat that's out of plane shows. A color match that's a quarter-shade off shows in the afternoon sun. We've been hand-troweling these walls long enough to know exactly what each pass needs to look like — and exactly how long to wait between them.

Whether you're patching a single crack or re-stuccoing the whole house, the work is the same in spirit: paper and lath properly fastened, scratch coat keyed in, brown coat floated true, finish coat troweled to match the texture of the original wall.

Color-integrated finishes mean no painting later.

For most jobs we recommend a color-integrated finish coat — pigment mixed into the top layer rather than applied as paint afterward. The benefit is simple: when the wall eventually chips or scrapes, the color underneath matches. There's no white plaster scar peeking out. We use La Habra, Omega, and Merlex finish products, with custom color matching from any chip you bring us.

Crack repair, done right

Most stucco cracks are cosmetic — settlement, thermal movement, or a hairline shrinkage from the original cure. We V-cut the crack, embed fiberglass mesh, fill in two passes, and texture-match the surrounding wall. For structural cracks we investigate the cause first — settling foundation, missing weep screed, water intrusion — because patching the symptom without fixing the source just kicks the problem six months down the road.

The finish coat is easy. The prep is everything.

Anyone can roll paint on a wall. We spend the hour before the brush comes out — washing, scraping, caulking, masking, priming — because that hour is what separates a paint job from a finish.

Paint that still looks new five years on.

The painters that disappear in the morning are easy. We're the ones still at your address in the afternoon, masking the floor before the rollers come out. For us, the paint is the cheap part — what costs is the time it takes to do it right. Pressure-washing the exterior. Scraping every bit of failing paint. Caulking gaps in the trim. Spot-priming bare wood. Sanding patches smooth. That's what makes a paint job last — not the brand on the can.

For interiors we use premium-grade acrylic latex from Sherwin-Williams or Benjamin Moore — Emerald, Cashmere, Aura, Regal. For exteriors we use 100% acrylic or elastomeric depending on substrate. Every job comes with a two-year workmanship warranty in writing.

Our prep checklist — the part nobody talks about.

For exteriors: pressure-wash to bare clean substrate, allow 48 hours dry time, scrape and feather all failing paint, sand glossy surfaces, spot-prime bare wood and stucco patches, caulk every joint and seam, mask windows, doors, lights, and landscaping, then two full finish coats — usually back-rolled after spray for a tighter bond.

For interiors: move and cover furniture, drop-cloth the floors, mask trim and floors, spackle nail holes and dings, sand smooth, prime patched areas, then two finish coats with proper between-coat dry times. Switch plates and outlet covers come off at the start and go back on at the end.

Two-year workmanship warranty

If a finish we applied fails through workmanship — peeling, blistering, premature flaking — within two years, we come back and fix it at no charge. We don't get many of those calls. We'd rather over-prep on day one.

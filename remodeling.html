Remodeling, thoughtfully done.

From a single bathroom refresh to a whole-home renovation, we approach every project with one question first — what does this house want to become?

A general contractor for the project that doesn't fit in a box.

Most remodels are not one trade. They're a kitchen that needs new electrical, fresh plumbing, new cabinets, refinished floors, and a fresh coat of paint — all sequenced so the painters aren't waiting on the plumbers and the floors don't get scuffed by the cabinet crew. That's the choreography a Class B General Contractor exists for.

We've remodeled mid-century ranches in Covina, Spanish revivals in Pasadena, and 1980s tract homes in West Covina. Every one is different. None are plugged into a template. We design the build around the house and the people in it — not the other way around.

What we remodel

  • Kitchens

    Layouts opened up, cabinets replaced or refaced, counters in stone or quartz, full appliance and lighting packages.

  • Bathrooms

    Tub-to-shower conversions, full tile work, glass enclosures, vanities, and fixture upgrades.

  • Room Additions

    Primary suites, family rooms, and second-story additions — engineered, permitted, and built end to end.

  • Accessory Dwelling Units

    Detached, attached, and garage-conversion ADUs designed under California's streamlined permitting codes.

  • Whole-Home Renovations

    Full down-to-studs renovations and historic restorations on a single coordinated timeline.

  • Garage Conversions

    Garages turned into offices, gyms, guest suites, or rentable units — engineered and code-compliant.

How a remodel actually goes.

The first conversation is free and on-site. We'd rather meet you in the kitchen you want to fix than try to estimate from photos. After that you receive a written, line-itemed proposal — with allowances called out clearly so you know exactly what's included before signing anything.

Once you approve, we pull permits, lock in a start date, and protect every adjacent surface before demo begins. From there it's daily site cleanup, weekly check-ins, and a single project manager you can text. We don't subcontract our way through the house — the same lead carpenter is there from frame to finish.

Materials we recommend, and why

We're not married to any one brand — we are married to the idea that a $200 faucet from a serious manufacturer will outlast three $80 ones. For cabinetry we lean on local mill shops we've used for years. For counters we'll send you to a slab yard ourselves rather than pick blind. The result is a house that holds up and a budget that doesn't get nickeled to death by callbacks two years later.

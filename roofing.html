The part you can't afford to get wrong.

A roof is a system — underlayment, ventilation, flashing, drainage. We install all of it the way it's drawn, so the inspector signs the first time and the rain stays where it belongs.

A roof that outlasts the warranty.

Most failed roofs don't fail because of bad shingles. They fail because of a lazy nail, an under-lapped underlayment, a vent boot that wasn't sealed, or a valley with no metal under it. We do roofing the slow way — the way it stays watertight for the full twenty-five or thirty years the manufacturer promises.

From a single leak repair after a storm to a full tear-off and re-roof, every job starts with an inspection from inside the attic and on the deck. We tell you what we find before we tell you what it costs.

Roofing systems we install

  • Architectural Shingle

    30 to 50-year laminated shingles in dimensional profiles, installed with synthetic underlayment and ice-and-water at all penetrations.

  • Concrete & Clay Tile

    Spanish, Mediterranean, and flat-profile tile — with double-layer underlayment and proper batten spacing for ventilation.

  • Modified Bitumen Flat

    Two-ply torch-down or self-adhered SBS systems for low-slope roofs, garages, and additions.

  • TPO & Single-Ply

    White, reflective single-ply membrane systems for cool-roof compliance on flat and low-slope assemblies.

  • Leak Repairs

    Targeted repairs to flashing, valleys, vents, and skylights — diagnosed and fixed without selling you a new roof.

  • Re-Roof & Tear-Off

    Full removal down to deck, deck repair as needed, complete reinstallation with permits and Title 24 documentation.

The details that separate a good roof from a bad one.

Synthetic underlayment, not 30-pound felt that tears underfoot. Ice-and-water shield in valleys and at every penetration, even though we're in Southern California. New flashing kits at chimneys and walls — never reused. Properly sized vent intake and exhaust so the attic actually breathes. Ridge cap nailed through the membrane, never just face-nailed and called good.

None of this costs much more than the cheap version. It just takes attention. That's the part we charge for.

Title 24 and cool-roof compliance

California's Title 24 energy code requires specific reflectance and emittance values for re-roofs in most climate zones. We handle the paperwork, pull the right permits, and choose tile or shingle products with verified CRRC ratings — so the inspector signs off without back-and-forth.
